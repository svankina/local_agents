Model hot-swap latency on a single GPU

How much do you pay to swap local models in & out of VRAM for a "route to the best model" fleet · RTX 3090 Ti (24 GB) · ollama 0.30.7 · weights on NVMe · 2026-06-13

The short answer

A swap-in costs a ~2–3 s fixed floor (spawn the runner, init the CUDA context) plus the weights — about size ÷ 2.5 GB/s warm in RAM, or size ÷ ~0.8 GB/s cold off NVMe. In practice: ~3 s for a small model, ~8 s for the 35B warm, up to ~27 s cold. But the real lever isn't disk — it's VRAM headroom. Small models co-reside and swap for free; the moment a big model is resident it evicts everything and inflates the next load to 17–30 s. Plan the fleet around what fits together, not just per-model load time.

Key findings

  • Page cache is the #1 variable. The 35B MoE loads in ~8.5 s warm but ~27 s cold off disk (once observed at 60 s under memory pressure). With 62 GB RAM your hot models stay cached between swaps — so warm numbers are what a busy router actually sees.
  • Small models ride together. ollama kept 3–4 models co-resident (1.5B+2B+3B+3.8B ≈ 7 GB). Routing among those triggers no eviction at all — swap cost ≈ the resident floor (sub-second to ~3 s).
  • Big models are jealous. Loading the 27B/35B evicts the entire pool. Worse, while a 22 GB model stays resident, loading anything else competed for VRAM and ballooned a 3B load from ~3 s to 17–27 s.
  • The 27B dense is the worst citizen — 16–35 s even warm, high variance. The 35B MoE loads faster (~8.5 s) despite being larger: fewer effective layers to wire up.
  • Eviction itself is cheap (<1 s); the cost is re-loading the incoming model, not unloading the outgoing.

What this means for a "best-model-for-the-job" fleet

  • Tier 1 — keep small specialists hot. A 1.5B–4B router/triage/extract pool (~7 GB total) co-resides permanently. Switching among them is free. This is where high-QPS routing lives.
  • Tier 2 — one big model, swapped deliberately. Reserve the remaining ~16 GB for one heavyweight (27B/35B) loaded on demand. Budget ~8–10 s warm / ~27 s cold per swap-in, and expect it to evict the small pool — so don't interleave big and small requests tightly.
  • Batch by model, don't ping-pong. 50 requests grouped by model pay the swap once; the same 50 interleaved pay it 50×. Route-then-batch beats route-per-request.
  • Keep weights in RAM. Cold-disk triples the penalty. With 62 GB RAM, a working set of ~5–6 models stays page-cached — keep it under RAM and you live on the warm numbers.
  • Rule of thumb: swap penalty ≈ 2.5 s + model_GB / 2.5 warm (the fixed term is runner spawn + CUDA init; it dominates for small models, bandwidth dominates above ~10 GB). If that's > your per-request latency budget, the model belongs in the always-resident tier, not the swap tier.
