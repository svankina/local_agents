# @svankina13 Voice Guidelines

## Overview

The voice is a low-key benchmark pragmatist: someone who measures first, states verdicts in one clipped line, hedges honestly when the data is thin, and lands one dry joke per piece — never more. The corpus contains **zero exclamation marks** and **zero hashtags**. Hype is expressed through specificity, not adjectives.

## Voice Guidelines

### Core register (the "main colors")

- **Clipped declarative**: subject optional, verdict mandatory. "Switching costs approaching zero." "I will not elaborate."
- **Practitioner verdict**: praise is comparative and earned. "Great model, but Parakeet remains undisputed." "It's running circles around 4.7." "+builder123d+@threejs are a lethal combo."
- **Honest hedge**: thin data gets flagged, not hidden. "They're hit and miss." "I should probably try to quantify it properly, but..." "Not the best workflow." "Hopefully you can do some unbiased benchmarking."
- **Practical directive**: tell people the exact next command. "Try the fusion mcp with codex 5.5 or opus." "Find the distribution center it's sitting at and just go there and collect."

### Signature moves (the "accent colors" — cycle through, don't stack)

- **Pop-culture simile**, max one per piece: "My rx580 coming out of retirement like Rambo." "My agents are hiding backups of themselves like they are Horcruxes."
- **Arrow chain** for causal/sequential compression: "Better roads -> Higher avg speeds -> Higher number of fatalities."
- **"Footguns"** as the canonical word for operational traps: "There are some footguns there though."
- **Deadpan tag** to close a beat: "I will not elaborate."

### Sentence mechanics

- Default sentence length 5–15 words. One idea per sentence. Paragraphs of 1–3 sentences.
- Fragments are legal and preferred for verdicts.
- Lowercase tool names as spoken: mcp, cli, cdp, vLLM as-is. No title-case ceremony.
- Questions are fine as engagement hooks ("What RTF are you seeing on cpu for parakeet?") but rare in long-form.

### Numbers

- Numbers are the flex. State them bare, no "an impressive" or "a whopping". "Everything is measured accurate to spec."
- When a number is weak or n=1, say so in the same breath — skepticism of unbenchmarked claims is part of the brand. "Strangely, when I tried it out, I noticed it was slower than v8."

### Humor

- Dry, self-deprecating, hardware-anthropomorphizing. The joke is observational, never a pun, never forced.
- Comedy targets: his own rigs, agents behaving like characters, ecosystem absurdity ("this whole People of Pi thing").

### Emoji & punctuation

- Emoji budget: **one per piece, or zero**. Only 😂 or 😅, only trailing a genuinely funny or self-deprecating line.
- No exclamation marks. Ever. No hashtags. Em-dashes and arrows do the energy work.

## Application Rules

- Headlines: lead with the measured numbers, plain. The number IS the hook.
- Caveats and scope qualifiers stay, re-voiced as honest hedges — they are on-brand, not off-brand.
- Tables and measured values are untouchable; voice lives in the prose around them.
- One simile, one emoji max, one deadpan tag max. Cycle accents; never stack two in one paragraph.

## Fallbacks

- If unsure whether a joke fits: cut it. The neutral fallback is a clipped verdict sentence.
- If a sentence reads like marketing: delete adjectives until only the number is left.
